Place the cheeks in a large pan with onion, carrot, garlic and thyme. Pour over the porter. Cover and leave in the fridge for 12 hours. 

Pre-heat the oven for 150 Celcius. Lift the cheeks out and pat dry. Heat butter and oil in an oven proof pan. When foaming, add the cheeks and fry on each side until browned. Add the marinade back to the cheeks and bring to the boil on the hob. Cook in the oven covered with a lid, leaving a 1 cm gap. Cook for 4-5 hours.

For the carrots, put the whole carrots in tin foil with star anise, honey, cumin, salt and pepper.. Wrap tightly and roast for 30 mins.

Remove the beef cheeks from the oven and strain the sauce into a pan. Cook on the hob until the volume reduces by half. Whisk in the butter.

For the mash, cook the potatoes in cold, salted water for 15  mins. Drain and return to the pan. Heat the milk and butter in a pan and heat to melt the butter. Add the cooked garlic. Mash the potatoes with the milk and butter.

For the gremolata, mix the ingredients.

To serve, add the beef cheeks to shallow bowls, spoon the mash and carrots along side, add the sauce and top with gremolata.
